Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the ability to use technology and the internet safely, responsibly, and respectfully. It involves a broad spectrum of practices, from protecting personal privacy to engaging in online communities with empathy and critical thinking.

What is Digital Law?

Digital law refers to the legal requirements and ethical standards that govern behavior in the digital world. It establishes electronic responsibility for actions and identifies what is permitted or prohibited online.

What is AUP?

AUP stands for Acceptable Use Policy. It is a formal document that outlines the rules, guidelines, and expectations for using an organization's technology resources, such as computers, networks, and the internet.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is the act of using another person's words, ideas, or intellectual property without proper attribution, presenting them as your own original work.

It doesn't matter whether it's done on purpose or by accident-it's still plagiarism

What is Academic Dishonestt?

Academic dishonesty is an umbrella term for any act that misrepresents a person's academic work or provides an unfair advantage in an educational setting. While plagiarism is a specific type of academic dishonesty, the broader category includes a wide range of deceptive behaviors.

What are the rules for AI generated content?

In 2026, academic rules for AI-generated content (AIGC) are primarily governed by the principle of human accountability, where students and researchers are solely responsible for the accuracy and integrity of their work, regardless of whether AI was used to assist in its creation.
