Academic Writing

English 1010

English 1010, often titled "Introduction to Academic Writing or "English Composition I," is an introductory college-level course designed to develop foundational writing, reading, and critical thinking skills. It is typically a required core course for freshmen.

In an English 1010 course, you are typically required to complete three to five major formal essays that emphasize writing as a recursive process of drafting and revision. These core projects often include a literacy narrative reflecting on your personal history with language, a rhetorical analysis that breaks down how specific texts persuade their audience, and a research-based argumentative or informative essay using external sources. Alongside these major papers, you must participate in mandatory "low-stakes" activities, such as peer review workshops where you provide feedback to classmates, and information literacy exercises like creating annotated bibliographies using MLA format. The semester frequently concludes with a final portfolio or reflection essay where you curate and revise your best work to demonstrate your growth as a writer.
